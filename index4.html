Featured Dishes

Eating is an ongoing and collective hobby in Nanjing, judging by the number of food stalls on one block and the ever-present aroma of duck soup through the city.

Tangbao (汤包) Duck soaked in brine (盐水鸭) blood soup (鸭血粉丝汤)
These steamed dumplings are sublime. Under an extremely thin layer of dough hides a bomb of delectable broth and a ball of tender pork. To eat these jiggly meat pockets, bite a small “window”on the skin, drink up the broth, dip the rest in vinegar, then munch. A most classic Nanjing food. This cold dish exemplifies Nanjing chef’s unparalleled skills in cooking the fowl. The duck meat is well-seasoned, juicy and tender. Bones are easy to get rid of. To make the dish, free-range ducks are first simmered in brine, hung to dry for around three days and then served as chops. Cubes of duck blood come with duck-bone stock with deep-fried firm tofu, vermicelli and bits of duck organs, such as liver and gizzard. The texture of the coagulated blood resembles bean curd: succulent, watery and tender. This soupy snack is a Nanjing food staple. Locals would order a bowl any time of the day.
Small Wan-Tan(小馄饨) River crab (小龙虾) Dumplings in red-bean soup (赤豆元宵)
These small things, loaded with minced beef or pork mixing diced ginger and spring onion, are cooked until swim on the water. The fragrant crescents provide a contrasting taste sensation: the outside is so thin while the inside is moist and soft. Every autumn between mid-September and mid-October, fishermen throws a grand festival to celebrate the harvest season of the lake delicacies, the crabs will always only be a trouble because they are everywhere, so the people try to wipe out them. The local way to enjoy these crustaceans is to cook them with different chinese herbal medicines and seasonings. Oh! This is absolutly my favorite dessert. This traditional dessert is rich but not overly sweet. The glutinous soup is made with sugared red bean paste and pearls of soft dumplings. Osmanthus honey is mixed in at the end. It is a popular food in Nanjing and east China and carries the lucky meaning of “family reunion.”
